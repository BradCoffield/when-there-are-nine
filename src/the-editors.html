Ashley Kunsa

Ashley Kunsa is assistant professor of creative writing at Rocky Mountain College in Billings, MT. She holds a PhD in English literature from Duquesne University in Pittsburgh, PA, and an MFA in fiction writing from Penn State. Ashley’s fiction, poetry, and creative nonfiction have been widely published in venues such as The Writer magazine, Blue Mesa Review, juked, the Los Angeles Review, and Sycamore Review. She is a recipient of the A Room of Her Own Foundation Orlando prize for flash fiction and tied for first place for the inaugural Experimental Essay Prize from Eastern Iowa Review. She lives in Billings with her husband and two small children.


Rebecca Evans

Rebecca Evans’ writing revolves around living with disability and overcoming trauma. She served eight years in the United States Air Force and is a decorated Gulf War veteran. She’s hosted and co- produced Our Voice and Idaho Living television shows, advocating personal stories, and now mentors teens in the juvenile system. She is the co-host of Writer to Writer podcast on Radio Boise. Her poems and essays have appeared in The Rumpus, Entropy Literary Magazine, War, Literature & the Arts, 34th Parallel, the Blue Mountain Review, Survival Lit, and Collateral Journal, among others. She holds an MFA in creative nonfiction and is finishing a second MFA in poetry at Sierra Nevada University. She lives in Idaho with her three sons.

Shaneen Harris

Shaneen A. Harris is a poet and programmer. She has published two books of poetry: Reflections of My Thoughts: I used to believe in unicorns, and I still do and Because God Said So , as well as Simply Complex, a spoken word CD. Her poem “Clergy Woman Eats Her Shadow” was a finalist in the Wolverine Farm Broadside Poetry Prize, and her pieces have appeared in Postcard Poems and Prose Magazine, Rubbertop Review, Crab Orchard Review: Cha-Ching the Money Edition, Watershed Review, and Rattle.

Shaneen received a BS in Electrical Engineering from Northwestern University, an MS in Electrical Engineering from Case Western Reserve University, and an MFA in Creative Writing poetry emphasis from Sierra Nevada University. She served as the host of Poetry Unchained , a web based poetry show, and TBN’s Joy in Our Town. Shaneen is married and the proud mother of 3 adult children.
