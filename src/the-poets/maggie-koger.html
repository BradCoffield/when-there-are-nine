Margaret Koger is a Lascaux Prize finalist and creative writing teacher from Boise, Idaho. As a National Endowment for the Humanities Teacher/ Scholar, she researched the interplay between nature and the economy during the settlement of the North American West. Her poetry appears in numerous journals including Amsterdam Quarterly, Collective Unrest, Thimble, Inez, Headway, Burning House, Voice of Eve, Tiny Seeds Literary Journal, and Ponder Savant.
